# PAWPAW WORLD User Manual

## Starting the Game

Run a local server (`python3 -m http.server 3000`) and open `http://localhost:3000`, or open `index.html` directly in a browser.

On the animated title screen choose **RESUME** to continue a saved city, or **START NEW** to begin a fresh wander. (START NEW confirms before erasing an existing save.) Progress saves automatically as you play.

## Controls

| Key | Action |
|---|---|
| Left / Right arrows | Move PawPaw |
| Up arrow | Jump |
| Space | Sprint |
| E | Interact with nearby objects and characters |
| S | Slide while sprinting or drop through platforms |
| X | Dash after it is unlocked |
| M | Open the city map |
| J | Open the journal |
| P | Enter or exit photo mode |
| Enter | Capture a photo in photo mode |
| Esc | Close menus and dialogs |

## The Three Zones

The city is explored across three connected, fully walkable layers. Each is filled with interactive spots:

- **Rooftops** — climb up via fire escapes, ladders, pipes, ziplines, and wires. Curiosities up here include rooftop pigeons, a sunlamp lounge, the garden bar, an observatory, a satellite dish, and the mast windsock.
- **Road / Street** — building ground floors are open storefront arcades, so you can walk the whole street as one continuous promenade past shops, stalls, and NPCs. Curiosities include the fish stall, scratch post, koi pond, busker tip jar, holo-mouse, rain puddle, midnight ramen, and a parked taxi.
- **Underground** — drop through street hatches into the tunnels for the Drowned Arcade, Line 9, the Hacker Den, glow mushrooms, the Rat Court, a drip pool, a tunnel mural, a broken jukebox, and a stray nest.

Walk up to any curiosity and press `E` to interact. Each gives kibble, credits, or XP, plays a sound and a short flavor line, then cools down so you can return to it later.

## What to Do

Explore the city at your own pace. There is no combat and no hard failure. Most activities give credits, XP, collectibles, cosmetics, shrine progress, or completion progress.

Good short-session goals:

- Collect a few holo-kibble.
- Complete one courier job.
- Chase one data wisp.
- Hack one terminal.
- Take one photo target.
- Find a secret room.

Good longer-session goals:

- Improve race medals.
- Rescue kittens.
- Rebuild the Paw Shrine.
- Complete NPC quests.
- Unlock skill tree perks.
- Chase District Completion.

## Journal Tabs

Open the city map with `M`. Open the journal with `J`.

- Quests: NPC quest chains, races, and courier streak.
- Completion: total District Completion percentage and every tracked category.
- Achievements: achievement checklist.
- Collection: collectibles, kittens, photo targets, landmarks.
- Skill Tree: spend upgrade points on Movement, Exploration, Economy, and Style perks.
- Cosmetics: current unlocked and equipped visual effects.
- Shrine: rebuild progress, shrine pieces, rescued kittens, and shrine upgrades.
- Race Records: medal targets, best times, and ghost replay status.
- Photo Challenges: photo target checklist.
- Stats: distance, jumps, credits, best streaks, races, kittens, secrets, and completion.

## XP, Levels, and Upgrade Points

Earn XP from collecting, racing, courier jobs, secrets, hacking, photos, kittens, wisps, quests, and flow combos. Leveling up gives:

- Credits
- One upgrade point
- A level-up reward popup
- A celebratory effect and sound

Spend upgrade points in the Skill Tree tab.

## Skill Tree

Movement helps flow and parkour.

Exploration improves discovery rewards and secret hints.

Economy improves courier payouts, flow payouts, vendor discounts, and streak protection.

Style unlocks cosmetic effects such as pawstep glow, jump sparkle, dash afterimage, and rare PawPaw skins.

## Vendors

Street vendors sell movement upgrades, economy upgrades, cosmetics, photo filters, shrine decorations, and consumable Synth-Tuna Snacks.

Credits come from almost everything: collectibles, flow combos, races, courier jobs, wisps, secrets, capsules, city events, and quests.

## Courier Jobs

Use courier boards around the city. Jobs are repeatable and have route labels:

- Quick
- Medium
- Long

Completing jobs in a streak increases payout. Better economy upgrades improve rewards and can protect a streak from one expired job.

## Races and Ghost Replay

Start races at glowing race rings. Each race has bronze, silver, and gold targets. After you complete a race, your best route is saved locally. On the next attempt, a transparent ghost PawPaw shows your best path so you can race yourself.

Improving a medal or best time gives better rewards and a celebration.

## Paw Shrine and Kittens

Lost kittens have names and personalities. Rescue them and carry them to the Paw Shrine. Rescued kittens appear at the shrine.

The shrine can be rebuilt with shrine pieces, credits, rescued kittens, rare collectibles, and story progress. Shrine upgrades unlock rewards, lore, cosmetics, passive exploration help, photo/style rewards, and a livelier shrine scene.

## Secrets and Mysteries

Secret rooms are hidden around rooftops, underground spaces, special landmarks, and unusual paths. They contain mystery rewards, shrine pieces, badges, lore fragments, and rare unlocks.

City mysteries slowly reveal what happened to Line 9, the ghost train, the scattered AI, PawPaw's connection to the city, and the Paw Shrine.

## Photo Mode

Press `P` to enter photo mode. If a target is in frame and its condition is active, the game marks it. Press `Enter` to capture. Targets include landmarks, the ghost train, a rescued kitten shrine moment, a rooftop jump, and rainy Neon Market. Completed photo targets reward credits and XP.

## Sound

Audio is fully procedural and adapts to where you are. Each zone has its own evolving music mood, weather adds rain and wind, and PawPaw has soft paw-tap footsteps that change with speed, metallic rung ticks while climbing ladders, and a warm purr when resting. Fever (a high flow combo) brightens and speeds up the music. Sound starts when you enter the city.

## Saving

Everything important saves automatically in localStorage. Closing the browser and returning later keeps progress, collectibles, race ghosts, upgrades, cosmetics, shrine progress, and completion data.
